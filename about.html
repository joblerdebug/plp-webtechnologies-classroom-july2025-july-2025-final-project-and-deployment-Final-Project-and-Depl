Our Story

From discovering a critical problem to creating a sustainable solution for African schools

The Discovery

During visits to boarding schools across Kenya, we witnessed firsthand the dangerous and costly reliance on firewood. We saw staff risking their health daily, schools spending fortunes on alternative fuels like lpg (liquid petroleum gas), and resources being diverted from education.

The Realization

We understood that firewood was more than just a cost issue , it was a safety, health, and operational crisis affecting the entire educational ecosystem. The solution had to address all these dimensions.

The Turning Point

Watching staff struggle with firewood at 5 AM risking their health ,and spending a lot of time preparing firewood, we knew we had to create a sustainable alternative. This moment solidified our commitment to transforming school energy systems.

The Solution

After more than a year of intensive research and programs, we developed the AfriBio solution: automated systems that safely convert school waste into clean cooking gas, eliminating firewood dependence.

The Problem We Solve

Three critical challenges facing African boarding schools today

Financial Drain

Schools spend up to KES 250,000 per term on firewood alone.money that should be redirected to crucial activities

Human Cost

Kitchen staff face daily risks including injuries, and long-term health issues from toxic smoke inhalation leave alone the sanitation issue.

Educational Impact

Precious resources are diverted from learning to survival, compromising the quality of education for thousands of students.
